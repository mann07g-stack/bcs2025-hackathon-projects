Welcome to the Amalfi Coast

Ethnicity & Culture

The Amalfi Coast is a living mosaic of southern Italy’s rich heritage. Its people are primarily Italian, with centuries of Greek, Roman, and Arabic influence shaping language, architecture, and cuisine. Local festivals celebrate ancient maritime traditions, and music ranges from folk tarantellas to modern Italian ballads.

Diversity & Hospitality

From fishermen in pastel villages to artisans crafting hand-painted ceramics, diversity thrives in daily life. Visitors will encounter a welcoming community proud of its multi-layered history and eager to share homemade limoncello, fresh seafood, and stories of the sea.

Why Tourists Love Amalfi

Dramatic cliffs plunge into turquoise waters, medieval churches guard winding alleys, and lemon groves scent the Mediterranean breeze. Whether you cruise the coastline, hike the Path of the Gods, or sip espresso on a sun-drenched terrace, every moment feels like a postcard come alive.

Plan Your Visit

Best season: Late spring to early autumn for warm weather and vibrant local festivals. Fly into Naples International Airport, then enjoy a scenic drive or ferry ride to Amalfi’s heart.
